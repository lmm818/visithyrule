Travel Tips
Since the calamity, Hyrule operates a little differently from other countries. There are monsters lurking about the land, sometimes in encampments. Due to that, we have consulted famous Hylian investigative journalist Traysi to create a list of important tips to keep your vacation safe, fun, and relaxed.
Tips for Humans
by Traysi
Investigative Journalist
Hi there! My name is Traysi, author of the famous #1 best-seller Rumor Mill. During my research in creating Rumor Mill, I traveled all over Hyrule. I learned and got used to a lot of different things, some were for my survival and others just neat tricks. Here are my top 10 tips for navigating this beautiful country.

★ Bring plenty of food to eat, pen and paper to draw or write about your travels, and an attitude for an adventure! Being mentally and physically prepared for a trip makes it go that much more smoothly.

★ I recommend an insulated bag for food which makes sure the weather does not spoil it.

★ Make sure to carry bananas at all times. Some people who look like travelers are actually part of the Yiga Clan. They will go away if you offer them a banana or two because it's their favorite snack!

★ If you spot black and pink goo, do not touch it! Trust me, it hurts to touch and it is not a fun time.

★ If you see a monster, pause, and let it walk by. If it spotted you, run to the nearest stable! If it gets to you, be prepared to give up some meals you’ve packed.

★ Stay away from Hyrule Castle and its immediate surrounding areas unaccompanied. You will not make it out.

★ Make sure to purchase Goron Spice from Goron City, it makes for the best flavoring in dishes.

★ If you will be traveling a lot, I recommend renting a horse from our many stables.

★ Elixirs will come in handy! It is ideal to keep a few different ready since you can use them even if you are full.

★ Have fun! Be in the moment and enjoy what Hyrule has to offer with the people you love.
